# Product Direction

Updated on `2026-06-01`

## Core Goal

`archiguide-kr` should become a light, bilingual architecture archive that helps architects browse notable buildings quickly without the weight of a large institutional portal.

The experience should feel closer to:

- `architectuurgids.nl` for speed, clarity, and archive-first navigation

and intentionally avoid the heavier interaction model of:

- `AURUM BuildingKakaoMap`

## Primary Audience

- Korean architects looking for recent references
- International architects who want an accessible entry into Korean architecture
- Students and researchers who need a browsable project archive rather than a search-engine-only database

## Initial Curation Rule

The first structured dataset should focus on:

- recently completed or recently recognized buildings
- projects by well-known or highly watched architecture offices
- projects that other architects would realistically reference

The first acquisition track starts with:

- winners of the `젊은건축가상 (Korean Young Architect Award)`

## Data Priorities

The project is not centered on awards themselves.

Awards matter as metadata, not as the main browsing axis.

That means:

- buildings should be browsed by architect, office, city, type, and country group
- award history should appear inside the building detail or project metadata
- the archive should not feel like an award site

## Award Intake Direction

Award collection should be organized by year.

The target structure is:

- collect all tracked awards for `2025`
- then all tracked awards for `2024`
- then move backward year by year as needed

This creates a more reliable archive than finishing one award across every year before touching the next.

## Browsing Axes

The first stable browsing axes should be:

- Buildings
- Architects
- Offices
- Korean architects
- International architects
- Cities
- Types
- Map

## Tone

- light
- fast
- editorial
- black text on white background
- minimal chrome
- no heavy portal feeling
- no institutional dashboard density

## UX Principles

- The homepage should remain a simple entry surface with featured buildings in sequence.
- The first screen should be readable in seconds.
- The interface should privilege scanning over interaction complexity.
- The map should remain secondary, not the first or dominant gesture.
- Filters should exist, but never make the archive feel bureaucratic.

## Data Expansion Order

1. Young Architect Award winners and their projects
2. Other notable Korean offices and recent buildings
3. International offices with relevant Korean or referenceable projects
4. Building details with awards, publications, and source links

## Current Interpretation

At this stage, `archiguide-kr` is best treated as:

- a curated architecture reference archive
- a building-first knowledge system
- a bilingual browsing tool

not as:

- a government portal
- a pure GIS product
- an awards microsite

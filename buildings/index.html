01

Seoul / Mapo

Floating Courtyard Library

Studio Layer, Field Office Seoul

A compact public library organized around a raised courtyard and a porous ground floor facing the street market.

The project tests how a civic building can behave like an urban shortcut during the day and a reading room at night.

2021LibraryBuiltBrick, Exposed concrete
02

Seoul / Seongsu

Seongsu River Workshop

Field Office Seoul

An industrial shell converted into a public workshop, cafe, lecture hall, and fabrication floor.

Instead of erasing the factory rhythm, the retrofit keeps the long-span frame visible and programs it as infrastructure.

2023Mixed UseAdaptive reuseSteel, Polycarbonate
03

Jeonju / Wansan

Hanok Climate Lab

Atelier Gonggan, North Wall Lab

A research center that reframes hanok courtyards as environmental devices rather than heritage replicas.

The plan uses layered thresholds, deep eaves, and a thermal buffer hall to turn climate into spatial experience.

2020Research CenterBuiltTimber, Clay tile
04

Busan / Yeongdo

Port Light Culture Hall

Coastal Common

A neighborhood culture hall that frames the harbor through stepped foyers and a lantern-like upper volume.

The building becomes a nighttime marker without turning into spectacle, relying on massing and reflected light instead.

2019Culture HallBuiltConcrete, Anodized aluminum
05

Daejeon / Yuseong

Forest Terrace Housing

North Wall Lab

A mid-rise housing prototype with shared winter gardens, study terraces, and a porous first-floor commons.

The key idea is not density alone but the quality of repeated shared edges where residents can actually linger.

2022HousingBuiltPrecast concrete, Galvanized steel
06

Daegu / Jung

Junction Market Commons

Field Office Seoul, Studio Layer

A former logistics shed reopened as a market commons with food stalls, archive rooms, and public seating terraces.

It treats commerce, memory, and climate shelter as one system instead of separating them into different zones.

2018MarketAdaptive reuseSteel truss, Terrazzo
07

Jeju / Aewol

Basalt Civic Hall

Coastal Common

A coastal civic hall composed as a cluster of basalt-toned rooms around a wind-protected gathering court.

The design avoids a single monumental facade and instead builds identity through repeated sheltered edges.

2024Civic HallUnder constructionBasalt aggregate concrete, Timber
08

Andong / Pungcheon

Seowon Study Archive

Atelier Gonggan

A small archive that alternates between exhibition rooms, reading alcoves, and a quiet open-air loggia.

The sequence is slow on purpose, asking visitors to move through light, slope, and silence before reaching the collection.

2017ArchiveBuiltTimber, Brick
